Tips for Crypto Beginners

  • Start with Litecoin (LTC). It has lower fees and faster confirmation times than Bitcoin. Good for smaller deposits.
  • Always double-check addresses. Crypto transactions cannot be reversed. One wrong character in a wallet address means your funds are gone.
  • Use a reputable exchange. Coinbase, Kraken, and Crypto.com are regulated US exchanges. Avoid unknown or offshore platforms.
  • Keep some crypto for gas fees. Ethereum transactions require "gas" fees. Don't send your entire ETH balance or you won't have enough for the transaction fee.
  • Track your transactions. Save transaction IDs (TXIDs) for all deposits and withdrawals. If anything goes wrong, you'll need these for support requests.

What Could Be Better

The crypto-only model has clear drawbacks:

  • High barrier to entry. If you don't own crypto, you need to sign up for an exchange, verify your identity, buy crypto, and then transfer it to Shuffle.us. That's significantly more friction than entering a credit card number on Stake.us.
  • Crypto volatility. The value of your deposit can change between the time you send it and when it confirms. A $50 Bitcoin deposit could be worth $48 or $52 by the time it lands in your account.
  • No fiat fallback. If you run into issues with your crypto wallet or exchange, there's no alternative way to fund your account. Other platforms like Stake.us let you switch to credit card if crypto isn't working.
  • Tax complexity. Crypto transactions may have tax implications. Converting between coins, depositing, and withdrawing all create taxable events in many jurisdictions. Consult a tax professional.

Crypto vs Fiat: Which Is Better for Sweepstakes?

Crypto advantages: faster transactions, greater privacy, lower processing fees, no chargebacks, and no bank involvement. If you value speed and privacy, crypto is genuinely better.

Fiat advantages: no extra setup, familiar payment methods, no volatility risk, simpler tax reporting, and accessible to everyone. If convenience matters most, fiat is the way to go.

For most casual players, fiat-friendly platforms like Stake.us are more practical. For crypto enthusiasts, Shuffle.us is purpose-built for you.
