What Are Sweepstakes Casinos?

A complete educational guide to how sweepstakes casinos work, their legal framework, and what makes them different from traditional platforms.

Sweepstakes casinos are platforms that use a dual-currency model to let US players enjoy casino-style games legally in most states. They operate under the no-purchase-necessary sweepstakes framework rather than traditional gambling licenses. Instead of using real money directly, players use virtual currencies: Gold Coins for entertainment and Sweeps Coins that can be redeemed for real prizes. This model has grown significantly since 2024, with dozens of platforms now serving millions of players across the United States.

How the Model Works

The sweepstakes casino model follows a specific structure designed to comply with US sweepstakes law. Players sign up for free, receive virtual currency, play casino-style games, and can redeem prize-eligible coins once playthrough requirements are met. The entire process is built around a promotional sweepstakes framework rather than a traditional gambling license. For a detailed step-by-step breakdown, see our How Sweepstakes Casinos Work guide.

The Dual Currency System

Every sweepstakes casino operates on two separate currencies. Gold Coins (GC) are free-play tokens used purely for entertainment with no real-world value. Sweeps Coins (SC) are the prize-eligible currency that can be redeemed for cash or other prizes, typically at a rate of $1 per SC. You receive both through sign-up bonuses, daily logins, promotions, and as bonus additions to Gold Coin package purchases. Learn how the system works in detail in our Dual Currency Explained guide.

Free Entry: Mail-In and AMOE

US sweepstakes law requires that every sweepstakes offer a free method of entry, known as the Alternate Method of Entry (AMOE). At sweepstakes casinos, this typically means mail-in requests: you send a handwritten request to the platform's designated address and receive Sweeps Coins at no cost. Most platforms also offer free Sweeps Coins through daily logins and social media promotions. Our Mail-In Entry Guide explains exactly how to format and send your request.

Legal Status by State

Sweepstakes casinos are legal in the majority of US states because they operate under sweepstakes promotional law, not gambling statutes. However, a few states restrict or prohibit them, including Washington, Idaho, and Montana. State-specific rules can also affect the minimum age requirement (18+ or 21+ depending on location). For complete details, check our Legal States Guide or explore the interactive State Legality Map.

How Sweepstakes Casinos Differ from Traditional Gambling

The sweepstakes casino model has several key differences from traditional platforms:

  • No purchase necessary: Every platform must provide a free way to obtain Sweeps Coins. You never need to spend money to play or win prizes.
  • Dual currency system: Instead of using real money directly, sweepstakes casinos use Gold Coins (entertainment) and Sweeps Coins (prizes). Traditional platforms use a single real-money balance.
  • Sweeps Coins are redeemable for prizes: Rather than direct cash payouts from gameplay, you accumulate Sweeps Coins and redeem them for prizes after meeting playthrough requirements.
  • Legal in most states: Because they operate under sweepstakes law rather than gambling regulations, these platforms are available in 47+ states. Traditional real-money platforms are licensed in only a handful of states.
  • Promotional framework: Gold Coin purchases are treated as buying a virtual product with bonus Sweeps Coins included as a promotional gift, not as placing real-money wagers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a sweepstakes casino?

A sweepstakes casino is a platform that offers casino-style games (slots, table games, live dealer) using a dual-currency system. Gold Coins are used for entertainment with no real value, while Sweeps Coins can be redeemed for real prizes. They operate under US sweepstakes law, which requires a free method of entry and no purchase to participate.

Are sweepstakes casinos legal?

Yes, in most US states. Sweepstakes casinos operate under the sweepstakes promotional model rather than gambling statutes, making them legal in 47+ states. Washington, Idaho, and Montana currently restrict or prohibit sweepstakes casino platforms. Always check your state's specific rules before signing up.

Do I need to purchase anything to play?

No. Every sweepstakes casino is required by law to offer a free method of entry. You can get Sweeps Coins through daily login bonuses, social media promotions, mail-in requests, and other no-cost methods. Purchasing Gold Coin packages is entirely optional.

How do I get prizes from a sweepstakes casino?

You accumulate Sweeps Coins through free methods or as bonuses included with Gold Coin purchases. After meeting the platform's playthrough requirement (typically 1x to 3x), you can redeem your Sweeps Coins for prizes. Most platforms offer bank transfers, cryptocurrency, and gift cards as redemption options.

What is the age requirement?

Most sweepstakes casinos require you to be at least 18 years old. However, some platforms and certain states set the minimum at 21. You will need to verify your identity (KYC) before redeeming any prizes, so your age and location will be confirmed during that process.
